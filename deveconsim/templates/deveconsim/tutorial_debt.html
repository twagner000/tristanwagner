Occasionally, your country will take on debt to pay for certain things. For example, you may decide to accept the World Bank's offer to lend you money to plant cocoa. The Debt page is where you can come afterwards to pay them off.

You started out the game with $1 billion in loans, left over from your predecessor's attempt to run the country. In this game, you must make annual interest payments on all of your accumulated debt but are not required to pay back any principal.

Because the World Bank's mission is to help developing countries, they will give you a lower interest than private banks – but they will only offer to fund projects that they believe will help your country.

If you have extra cash, it would be a good idea to pay off some of your country's debt.

Business Consultant

"Your country desperately needs to grow its economy – and a loan can help! As long as your country makes more money than the interest you pay, you'll have gotten a great deal. The World Bank is usually generous, as long as the money goes to a worthy project."

Social Activist

"Loans will hurt you in the long run with their heavy interest payments. Do you really trust the World Bank to pick out which projects will help your country?"
